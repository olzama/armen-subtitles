**0\. RESUMO DO PROXECTO**

**Título do proxecto:** **CALMA: Conciliación y Algoritmos Logísticos para Mayores y Asistencia**

**Obxectivo Central:** CALMA é unha iniciativa *deep-tech* deseñada para revolucionar a xestión do persoal no sector da asistencia a maiores mediante o desenvolvemento dunha plataforma de optimización automatizada e de alto rendemento. O proxecto aborda a crise demográfica sistémica e a escaseza de man de obra na Unión automatizando a xeración de cadros de quendas rotatorios, cíclicos, xustos e legalmente conformes.

**Novidade Tecnolóxica:** No seu núcleo tecnolóxico, CALMA implementa un motor de hibridación neuro-simbólica novidoso. Esta arquitectura integra Grandes Modelos de Linguaxe (LLMs) para a extracción de restricións sociais e legais complexas a partir da linguaxe natural co rigor matemático da Programación Lineal (LP) para garantir o 100% de cumprimento das leis laborais. Ao utilizar a descuberta de heurísticas impulsada pola IA, a plataforma pretende aproximar solucións a problemas de programación NP-hard/NP-complete en tempo real, permitindo a reoptimización global durante alteracións imprevistas.

**Resultados Esperados e Impacto Socioeconómico:**

* Un prototipo funcional capaz de reducir o tempo administrativo de programación de quendas en máis dun 90%.  
* Mellora significativa na retención do persoal ao respectar solicitudes complexas de conciliación (dereitos sociais e preferencias importantes).  
* Un marco tecnolóxico escalable aplicable a outros sectores públicos de alta presión (saúde, seguridade) en toda a UE.

**1\. CONTIDO CIENTÍFICO-TÉCNICO DO PROXECTO**

a) Obxectivos Científico-Tecnolóxicos

O proxecto aspira a transcender as limitacións do software de programación de quendas actual conseguindo o seguinte:

* **Desenvolvemento dun Motor de Optimización neuro-simbólico**: Crear un sistema híbrido que combine a flexibilidade da IA Xenerativa para a extracción de restricións "brandas" (preferencias do persoal/tarefas non urxentes) co rigor matemático da Programación Lineal (LP) para o cumprimento legal "duro" (48h de descanso semanal, 12h de separación entre quendas, etc. e rotacións cíclicas). Un diferenciador central da arquitectura CALMA é o seu aliñamento nativo cos principios da **IA Explicable (XAI)** e os mandatos de transparencia da **Lei de IA da UE**. Ao adoptar un marco **neuro-simbólico**, o proxecto segrega a natureza de 'caixa negra' da IA Xenerativa—usada estritamente para a extracción flexible de solicitudes en linguaxe natural—da lóxica final de toma de decisións, que é gobernada por un motor simbólico de **Programación Lineal (LP)**. Isto asegura que todas as saídas de programación sexan **matematicamente rastrexables** e auditables. Debido a que o cumprimento legal (p.ex., 48h de descanso semanal, 12h de separación entre quendas) se impón mediante restricións simbólicas explícitas en lugar de patróns probabilísticos, o sistema pode proporcionar unha xustificación clara para cada asignación de quenda. Esta 'transparencia por deseño' é un requisito clave para os sistemas de IA de alto risco na xestión de traballadores, garantindo a rendición de contas e previndo o nesgo algorítmico no crítico sector da asistencia a maiores.  
* **Discovery of Synthetic Heuristics**: To utilize GenAI to synthesize massive datasets of complex scheduling scenarios, identifying and codifying new heuristics that allow for faster solutions to NP-hard optimization problems in real-time.  
* **Real-time Re-optimization Module**: To build a dynamic response system capable of re-calculating the entire staff matrix in seconds when faced with unforeseen disruptions (e.g., sick leave), ensuring continuous care coverage.  
* **Commercial & Social Outcomes**: To reduce the administrative burden on managers by 90% and increase staff satisfaction and retention by providing schedules that respect work-life balance and social rights.  
* **Timeline**: Achievement of a functional, field-tested prototype within 24–27 months.

b) Background and State of the Art

Despite the critical nature of the sector, the current state of scheduling in elderly care is characterized by three major gaps:

1. **The Manual Status Quo**: While the market offers various administrative tools, they fail to address the algorithmic complexity of the elderly care sector. Most residencias currently utilize generic HR and payroll suites—such as **Sage 200**, **A3Innuva**, or **SAP SuccessFactors**—which manage contracts and attendance but lack any automated shift-scheduling logic, leaving directors to manually solve NP-hard logistical puzzles on paper or Excel. Specialized scheduling solutions like **Lya2** exist **but are designed for hospital environments** with more stable staffing ratios and lack the **neuro-symbolic** flexibility required to handle the 'soft' social constraints (e.g., work-life balance requests) inherent to the overly feminine care sector. Alternatively, advanced AI platforms like the American-based **YouShift** are prohibitively expensive due to dollar-denominated SaaS pricing and lack the native integration of Spanish Labor Law and regional collective agreements. Strategic reliance on non-EU technology also conflicts with the **EU STEP** objectives of digital sovereignty. CALMA transcends these limitations by combining Generative AI for natural language constraint extraction with mathematical Linear Programming to ensure 100% legal compliance while significantly reducing administrative burden.  
2. **Demographic & Shortage Volatility**: While hospital-centric software exists, it is designed for environments with more stable staffing ratios (e.g., physicians). In elderly care homes, the demographic gap in Galicia has created a staffing crisis where the requirement/staff balance is extremely fragile. Optimization must occur at a **completely different rate** and depth to prevent service collapse.  
3. **The Socio-Legal Landscape**: Existing generic tools fail to account for the specific labor rights in Spain, such as reduced loads and accommodations for caretaking. Given that the workforce is overwhelmingly female, these "social constraints" are not outliers—they are the norm—and their exclusion from current software renders those tools useless for *residencias*.  
* **The Innovation**: Our project introduces a "Deep Tech" approach by using AI not just to *fill* a schedule—which will already be a revolutionizing innovation—but to *learn* the underlying heuristics of the care sector, providing a level of adaptability that hospital-based systems lack.

c) Current State of Development (TRL)

**Current Level (TRL 2/Early 3):** The project has successfully completed the **Strategic Discovery Phase**.

* **Stakeholder Engagement:** We have conducted extensive interviews with sector representatives (elderly care facilities and hospitals) to identify their most pressing needs.  
* **Data:** We are in the process of acquiring a representative corpus of real-world scheduling data and anonymized workforce profiles. This ensures that our initial modeling is grounded in the actual complexity of the sector's requirements rather than theoretical assumptions.  
* **Conceptual Validation:** We have a precise model of the main problem to solve in computer science and mathematical terms. and we have identified the specific neuro-symbolic integration points where Generative AI will interact with the Linear Programming engine.  
  **Target Level (TRL 7):** Through the execution of this project, we intend to move from this verified conceptual framework to a fully functional prototype demonstrated in an operational environment (a partner residency in Ferrol, Galicia).

d) Dimension of Innovation

* **Regional**: Directly addresses the Galician "Demographic Challenge" and the sustainability of its Silver Economy.  
* CALMA is strictly aligned with the **Estratexia de Especialización Intelixente (RIS3) de Galicia**, specifically within the priority **'Modelo de vida saudable baseado no envellecemento activo'**. By automating complex logistics in residencias, the project provides the technological infrastructure necessary to support an aging population, ensuring the sustainability of the Galician socio-sanitary system through digital transformation.  
* **European/Global**: The solution aligns with the EU STEP objectives by reducing administrative friction in critical healthcare infrastructure and is highly exportable to other aging EU nations (Germany, Italy).

e) Future Projection

Successful deployment in elderly care will provide a "blue-print" for other high-pressure, resource-constrained sectors including:

* Emergency and Law Enforcement services.  
* Education and complex laboratory staffing.  
* Public health system (Sergas) integration for specialized nursing units.

### **5.2. Technology Sector: Digital and Deep Tech**

The project develops and implements specific high-impact digital technologies:

* **AI Algorithms**: Research into new heuristics for NP-hard problems using Generative AI.  
* **Data Analysis Technologies**: Processing massive synthetic and real-world datasets to identify workforce patterns.  
* **NLP and Computer Vision**: Utilizing multimodal processing (Natural Language Processing) to transform unstructured worker requests into mathematically rigorous constraints.

### **5.3. Fundamental Technology Conditions (Art. 6.1)**

The technology is characterized as **emerging** and **cutting-edge**:

* **Emerging Elements**: The hybrid neuro-symbolic approach is a nascent field in computer science. It derives from the research base of the project leads (PhDs in Computational Sciences) and shows significant promise for transforming the "Care Economy".  
* **Cutting-edge Elements**: Unlike existing hospital-centric or manual solutions, **CALMA** represents the most advanced development in the Union specifically tailored for the high-volatility environment of elderly residencias.

### **5.4. Significant Economic Potential (Art. 6.2)**

The project demonstrates substantial potential for the internal market:

* **Market Scale**: While the pilot is situated in Galicia, the "aging crisis" is a Pan-European challenge. The solution is designed for rapid scalability to markets with similar demographic profiles, such as Germany and Italy.  
* **Indirect Effects**: By stabilizing the care sector workforce, the project generates positive spillover effects for the broader European economy, ensuring that the working-age population (specifically female caretakers) can remain in the labor market through better "conciliación" (work-life balance).

### **5.5. Reduction of Strategic Dependencies (Art. 6.3)**

The technology strengthens the Union's resilience in three key areas:

* **Industrial Leadership**: Establishing a European competitive advantage in specialized AI-driven logistics.  
* **Critical Infrastructure**: Elderly care facilities are essential social infrastructures. Automating their resilience protects the Union from systemic service collapses.  
* **Cross-border Effects**: The platform promotes the coordination of labor standards and efficiency across the internal market, reducing distortions in the quality of care services between Member States.

### **5.6. Technical Viability and Market Advancement (Art. 7 & 8\)**

* **Viability Validation**: The project’s viability will be demonstrated through a \~27-month R\&D roadmap. This includes stress-testing the engine with 20,000 unique synthetic NP-hard scenarios (Activity 1.2) and a 6-month operational pilot at a residency in Ferrol, Galicia (Activity 4.1).  
* **Market Alignment**: Activities are specifically geared toward refining the technology to meet rigorous market needs, including UI/UX A/B testing and compliance with the *Estatuto de los Trabajadores* and regional labor agreements.
