1. **"Бесы" by Fyodor Dostoevsky**: Pyotr Stepanovich Verkhovensky visits Kirillov at night, who is consumed by the idea of becoming a "man-God" through suicide. Verkhovensky plans to use Kirillov's death as an alibi for political murder. During their meeting, Verkhovensky consumes a boiled chicken intended as a metaphor, stripping Kirillov of his autonomy and life.

2. **"Don Quixote" by Miguel de Cervantes**: Don Quixote plans to mimic the knights of the past by going mad without reason. His squire, Sancho Panza, is overly focused on food. This dynamic mirrors Kirillov and Verkhovensky’s, illustrating a conflict between high ideals (Don Quixote/Kirillov) and base desires (Sancho/Verkhovensky).

3. **"Barabek, the Glutton"** (referenced in the text): Verkhovensky is metaphorically likened to Barabek, a gluttonous character who consumes almost anything, symbolizing his voracious ambition and disregard for others, including devouring Kirillov's last meal and metaphorically his life.
