TRANSLATION CASE RETRIEVAL SCHEMA
Derived from Beware of the Car (1966) translation workflow; adapt to any film.

This schema encodes resolved, instance-level translation knowledge.
It is meant for retrieval and conditioning, not rule enforcement.

--------------------------------------------------
ENTITY: TranslationCase
--------------------------------------------------

id:
  Unique identifier for the case.
  Example: BAC_06

film:
  Film title.
  Example: "Beware of the Car" (1966)

scene:
  Short description of the scene.
  Focus on situation, not plot summary.
  Example: "Dima panics at home, confronted by his father-in-law"

russian_line:
  Original Russian line as it appears in subtitles.
  Example: «— Что делать? Что делать? — Сухари сушить!»

english_translation:
  Final approved English translation.
  Example: “Pack a toothbrush.”

timecode:
  Approximate start–end time in the film.
  Example: 01:01:00–01:01:15

speaker:
  Character name.
  Example: "Semyon Vasilyevich"

speaker_profile:
  Compact description of the speaker’s social and performative profile.
  Include:
    - age / generation
    - relation to addressee
    - energy (e.g. energetic, blunt)
    - register (colloquial, bookish, official)
  Example:
    "Older male, father-in-law, energetic, blunt, mocking"

addressee:
  Who the line is directed at.
  Example: "Dima (son-in-law)"

pragmatic_function:
  What the line DOES in the scene (speech act).
  Examples:
    - dismisses panic
    - reassigns social status
    - blocks suspicion through trust
    - formulates cynical житейское правило

key_decisions:
  Bullet list of positive decisions that led to this translation.
  These are authoritative.
  Example:
    - Preserve clarity over literal imagery
    - Use culturally equivalent prison-preparation gesture
    - Avoid explanatory wording

rejected_options:
  Bullet list of translations or strategies explicitly rejected,
  with brief reasons.
  Example:
    - “Start drying bread” — opaque to non-Russian audience
    - “Get ready for jail” — flat, non-comic

construction_notes:
  Notes on grammar or construction that were critical.
  Example:
    - Russian «а ты не воруй» is not an imperative
    - English imperatives must be avoided here

keywords:
  Flat list of retrieval tags.
  Should include:
    - situation (panic, exposure, authority)
    - domain (prison, law, family)
    - speech-act type (dismissal, verdict, aphorism)
  Example:
    ["panic", "prison", "dismissal", "father-in-law"]

--------------------------------------------------
USAGE NOTES
--------------------------------------------------

• Retrieval is based on analogy, not rules.
• Retrieved cases are treated as authoritative precedents.
• Only a small number (2–4) of closest cases should be injected per API call.
• Explanations are optional; translations are primary.
• Negative knowledge (rejected options) is critical and must be preserved.

--------------------------------------------------
END OF SCHEMA
--------------------------------------------------
